Gondor

Minas Tirith – The White City and capital of Gondor, built into a mountainside with seven levels, symbolizing the last great strength of Númenórean civilization in the West.

Osgiliath – The ancient former capital of Gondor, straddling the Anduin River; mostly ruined and contested during the War of the Ring.

Minas Morgul – Once Minas Ithil, now a cursed city ruled by the Witch-king, glowing with unnatural light and serving as a stronghold of Mordor.

Pelargir – A major port city on the Anduin, crucial to Gondor’s naval power.

Dol Amroth – A coastal city and princedom, famed for its swan-knights and strong Elvish heritage.

Calembel, Linhir, Ethring – Smaller Gondorian towns supporting trade and defense.

Rohan

Edoras – The hilltop capital of Rohan, crowned by Meduseld, the Golden Hall of the Kings.

Aldburg – The original capital of Rohan and stronghold of the royal line.

Helm’s Deep (Hornburg) – A fortress rather than a city, but a key defensive refuge and population center in times of war.

Arnor (Ruined Northern Kingdom)

Annúminas – The ancient capital of Arnor near Lake Evendim, long abandoned.

Fornost – A later capital of Arnor, now in ruins and haunted by memory.

Tharbad – A former great trading city at a river crossing, reduced to decay.

The Shire

Michel Delving – The administrative center of the Shire, home of the Mayor.

Hobbiton – A well-known village symbolizing peaceful Hobbit life.

Bywater – A working-class village later scarred by Saruman’s occupation.

Tuckborough – Seat of the powerful Took family.

Bucklebury – A settlement guarding the Brandywine Bridge.

Eriador & the North

Bree – A rare mixed settlement of Men and Hobbits, vital as a crossroads town.

Rivendell (Imladris) – An Elven refuge hidden in a valley, more a sanctuary than a city.

Elven Realms

Caras Galadhon – The city of Lothlórien, built among the great mallorn trees.

Mithlond (The Grey Havens) – A western port from which Elves depart Middle-earth.

Dwarven & Allied Cities

Erebor (The Lonely Mountain) – A vast underground Dwarven city and kingdom.

Dale – A prosperous city of Men rebuilt near Erebor after Smaug’s fall.

Esgaroth (Lake-town) – A trading city built on wooden pylons over water.

Mordor & Enemy Strongholds

Barad-dûr – Sauron’s dark fortress, the center of his power.

Cirith Ungol – A tower guarding a key pass into Mordor.

Durthang – A western Mordor fortress overlooking Udûn.

Harad & Umbar

Umbar – A powerful southern port city, long hostile to Gondor and home to the Corsairs.
