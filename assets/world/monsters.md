Balrog - Ancient demons of fire and shadow from the First Age. The Balrog of Moria, also called Durin's Bane, is a massive humanoid wreathed in flame and darkness, wielding a fiery whip and sword. One of the Maiar corrupted by Morgoth.
Nazgûl (Ringwraiths) - Nine mortal kings corrupted by Sauron's rings of power, now existing as undead servants. They appear as shadowy figures in black robes, invisible without their cloaks, riding black horses or fell beasts. Their presence causes despair and terror.
Fell Beasts - Large, pterodactyl-like flying creatures ridden by the Nazgûl. Described as having naked skin, vast bat-like wings, skeletal forms, and reeking of decay.
Shelob - An enormous ancient spider dwelling in the pass of Cirith Ungol. Daughter of Ungoliant, she has existed since the First Age, with a bloated body, multiple eyes, and venomous fangs that paralyze victims.
Cave Troll - Large, thick-skinned humanoids used by orcs in Moria. They're strong but dim-witted, with scaly green hide resistant to weapons.
Watcher in the Water - A tentacled creature lurking in the pool outside Moria's west gate. Has many gripping tentacles and pulls victims underwater.
Barrow-wights - Undead spirits inhabiting the burial mounds of the Barrow-downs. They lure travelers into their tombs and attempt to sacrifice them with ancient swords.
Orcs - Corrupted humanoid creatures serving evil. Smaller and weaker than men but numerous, with various breeds including the larger Uruk-hai created by Saruman.
Gollum - While not traditionally a monster, this creature was once a hobbit-like being named Sméagol, twisted by centuries of possessing the One Ring into a wretched, amphibious creature.
